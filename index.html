What is Disc Golf?

Disc Golf has a history dating back to the 1960s. The disc or frisbee is patented by Ed Headrick who is also known as the Father of Disc Golf. You throw the disc toward the Disc Golf Pole Hole. Scoring is tracked like golf, the smaller the number the better.

Disc golf is a sport that combines the precision of traditional golf with the fun and excitement of frisbee. Players use specially designed discs to complete each hole in the fewest throws possible. Whether you're aiming for a long-distance drive or a delicate putt, disc golf offers a unique challenge for players of all skill levels.

There is also an app for Apple or Google store where you can keep track of your scores on all sanctioned courses, called UDisc

Iowa State Disc Golf

Iowa is a hidden gem in the disc golf world, offering some of the best courses in the Midwest. The state's diverse landscapes—from rolling hills to dense forests—provide the perfect backdrop for disc golf enthusiasts. Whether you're in a small town or a bustling city, you'll find a course that challenges your skills and showcases Iowa's natural beauty.

Some of Iowa Best Courses

  • Water Works Park, Des Moines: One of the most popular courses in the state, with its scenic views and challenging layout, it's a favorite among locals and visitors alike.
  • Sugar Bottom, North Liberty: Nestled in a wooded area near Coralville Lake, This course offers a technical challenge with its tight fairwars and elevation changes.
  • Pickard Park, Indianola: Known for its long-distance holes and beautiful scenery, Pickard Park is a championship-level course that has hosted numerous tournaments.
